About Me

I am a final-year PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Ghana, researching foundation model adaptation and generalized few-shot semantic segmentation. My work orchestrates multiple foundation models — CLIP, SAM, DINOv2, and Flan-T5 — in training-free pipelines that achieve state-of-the-art results.

Research

Investigating when and how to adapt foundation models at inference time — and when naive adaptation catastrophically fails.

BMVC 2026 · Submitting

Regime-Aware Test-Time Refinement for GFSS

Identifies two failure modes: catastrophic negative transfer (−28.7 pp) and unreliable SAM boundary refinement. Proposes TIPM with provable convex hull safety guarantees and CgS for CLIP-guided SAM point selection.

63.58% Harmonic Mean — PASCAL-5i 5-shot (DINOv2) — SOTA among all real-data GFSS methods
ICANN 2026 · Under Review

TIPM-Seg: Test-Time Iterative Prototype Mining

Formalises catastrophic negative transfer via a two-term distortion bound. Proves random initialisation eliminates both distortion terms in expectation, motivating inference-only adaptation.

52.3% Novel mIoU — PASCAL-5i 5-shot (ResNet-50) — surpasses VP, BAM, DIaM, CAPL

Key Theoretical Contributions

Convex Hull Safety Guarantee

Proved that softmax attention ensures refined prototypes lie within the convex hull of support features regardless of parameter values.

Two-Term Distortion Bound

Formal decomposition of negative transfer into attention mismatch and value projection mismatch, both vanishing under random initialisation.

Query-Conditioned Refinement

Even randomly initialised attention provides meaningful directional signal, drawing prototypes toward query-relevant support features.
