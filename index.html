Research Vision

Building adaptive foundation models that coordinate pretrained knowledge efficiently at inference time.

I am a final-year PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Ghana. My doctoral research develops a unified framework for Inference-Time Coordination, in which frozen foundation-model representations are selected, related, and resolved during inference without task-specific parameter optimisation.

The framework is instantiated across three domains: feature-space coordination for generalized few-shot semantic segmentation, geometric coordination for multi-view three-dimensional scene understanding, and semantic coordination for training-free open-vocabulary segmentation.

My current work extends this programme towards reinforcement learning for multimodal reasoning, with emphasis on process-level reward design, visual grounding verification, and efficient post-training of vision-language models.

Research Programme

Three doctoral contributions unified by one computational principle, followed by an emerging multimodal RL direction.

Feature-Space Coordination

TIPM-Seg: Test-Time Iterative Prototype Mining

Non-parametric prototype refinement for generalized few-shot semantic segmentation. The method coordinates support and query representations at inference time while avoiding dependence on a base-trained refinement network.

63.58%Harmonic Mean on PASCAL-5i 5-shot with a DINOv2 backbone.

Geometric Coordination

Cross-View Semantic Consensus for 3D Point Clouds

Training-free reconciliation of dense three-dimensional priors with sparse semantic observations lifted from calibrated image views. Cross-view evidence is aggregated per point to suppress inconsistent observations and recover geometrically coherent predictions.

Preprint StatusProject Repository
Semantic Coordination

OpenV-GFSS: Open-Vocabulary Semantic Arbitration

Training-free generalized few-shot segmentation through coordination of promptable segmentation proposals, vision-language embeddings, and few-shot support centroids. The method supports open-vocabulary inference without task-specific parameter updates.

arXivCode
Emerging Research

Multimodal Reinforcement Learning and Visual Grounding

Process-level reward design for vision-language reasoning, combining answer correctness, semantic matching, format validity, and grounding verification. Current experiments investigate GRPO and VERL-based post-training for multimodal foundation models.
